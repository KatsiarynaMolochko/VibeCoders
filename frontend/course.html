Lecture: Introduction to the Product Manager Role

12 minutes Theory Level: Beginner

A product manager connects users, business, and the development team. Their goal is to find valuable problems, validate solutions, and deliver outcomes that benefit users and the company.

Key responsibilities

  • Define product vision and shape the strategy.
  • Understand user needs through interviews, surveys, and data analysis.
  • Prioritize tasks based on value and complexity.
  • Communicate with stakeholders and keep the team aligned.

Values and metrics

Focus on user value. Metrics help measure progress and guide decisions. Separate metrics into product (activation, retention, engagement) and business (revenue, margin).

Tip

Start with a North Star Metric: one key metric that reflects the value users receive. All initiatives should move this metric.

Research process

  1. Form hypotheses about the user problem.
  2. Conduct qualitative interviews to uncover insights.
  3. Validate hypotheses with quantitative data.
  4. Identify user segments and their jobs-to-be-done.

Prioritization map

Use RICE or ICE frameworks to compare ideas, considering impact, confidence, and effort. Transparency of criteria keeps the team aligned.

RICE calculation example 
Reach: 2,000 users / quarter
Impact: 2 (moderate)
Confidence: 0.7
Effort: 2 person-weeks

RICE = (2000 * 2 * 0.7) / 2 = 1400

Summary

The product manager finds and validates value, then delivers it. Focus on user needs, measure outcomes, and iteratively improve the product.
